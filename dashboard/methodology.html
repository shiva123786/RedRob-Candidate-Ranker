How the score is built

Methodology

No embeddings model, no LLM call, no network access at rank time — by design. Every signal below is a deliberate, auditable rule traced back to a specific line in the job description. That's the tradeoff: full explainability and a ~2-minute, CPU-only run over the whole 100K-candidate pool, in exchange for not using a heavier model.

Pipeline

STAGE 1 · engine/jd_knowledge

Lexical relevance

Regex-matched concept buckets — embeddings/retrieval, vector & hybrid search, eval frameworks (NDCG/MRR/P@K), production language — scanned over headline, summary, every career-history description, and skill names. Hits are capped per bucket (BM25-style saturation) so repeating one buzzword stops paying out.

STAGE 2 · engine/features + scoring

Structured re-ranking

Title seniority, skill credibility (proficiency discounted by duration/endorsements/assessment score — this is what catches keyword-stuffing), career-industry quality, experience-band fit, location & notice-period logistics, education tier, and six explicit disqualifiers.

STAGE 3 · features._behavioral_modifier

Behavioral availability

Multiplicative modifier (0.4×–1.2×) from recency of activity, recruiter response rate, interview completion rate, open-to-work flag, and verification signals. Applied after content scoring — fit and availability are judged separately, then combined.

STAGE 4 · engine/honeypot

Honeypot filter

Hard exclude, not a down-weight. Three internally-consistent signals (detailed below) catch ~60 candidates with impossible profiles before they ever enter the ranking heap.

Explicit disqualifiers

Multiplicative penalties, not hard excludes — each maps to a "things we explicitly do NOT want" line in the JD. They stack if more than one fires.

services_only
Entire career confined to TCS / Infosys / Wipro / Accenture / Cognizant / Capgemini.
×0.25
cv_speech_dominant
≥3 computer-vision/speech skills with zero NLP/IR/retrieval evidence anywhere.

×0.20

How the ~60 honeypots were found

The dataset embeds candidates with internally-impossible profiles. Each heuristic below was verified against the full 100K-candidate population before use — on the legitimate population, the value is always near zero; on honeypots, it jumps straight into a separate cluster. That clean separation is what allows an exclude-on-any-match rule with no observed false positives.

expert-proficiency skill, ~0 months of use → 99,979 / 100,000 candidates: zero such skills → the ~21 that have any, have 3–5 simultaneously career_history duration_months vs. start/end dates → 99,965 / 100,000 candidates: within 3 months (rounding) → honeypot tail: 9–150+ months of inconsistency career_history total months vs. years_of_experience → 99,977 / 100,000 candidates: within 24 months → honeypot tail: starts at 25+ months over
