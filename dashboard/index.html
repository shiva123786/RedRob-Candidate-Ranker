How it thinks

Full methodology →
STAGE 1

Lexical relevance

Scans career history and skills against a hand-weighted vocabulary pulled directly from the JD — embeddings, retrieval, vector search, eval frameworks — with saturating caps so repeating one buzzword stops paying out.

STAGE 2

Structured re-ranking

Title seniority, career-industry quality, experience-band fit, location & notice-period logistics, education tier, and the JD's explicit disqualifiers (services-only career, CV/speech-only, stale-architect, LLM-wrapper-only…).

STAGE 3

Behavioral availability

A perfect-on-paper candidate who's been dark for six months and ignores recruiters isn't actually available. Recency, response rate, and engagement modulate the score up or down.

STAGE 4

Honeypot safety filter

~60 candidates with internally-impossible profiles (expert skills with zero months of use, career dates that don't add up) are excluded outright — never down-weighted, removed.
